STMA Cub Scouts is a group of local packs serving St. Michael, Albertville, and the surrounding area. From camping under the stars to building robots, we help young people discover their best selves. Frequently Asked Questions

Can't find an answer? Reach out with the contact form below and we'll get back to you.

Will my children be safe?
Scouting has the most comprehensive youth protection policies of any youth organization. Safety is the organization's #1 priority.
Is Scouting still relevant for modern families?
Yes — Scouting builds respect for others, character, fitness, and leadership. It prepares children for life.
What kinds of activities do Cub Scouts do?
Outdoor activities like camping, hiking, and archery; STEM activities including robotics and hands-on learning; family-oriented adventures; and crafts, Pinewood Derby races, and other fun projects.
Can girls join?
Yes — Cub Scouting is open to both boys and girls. It's designed for the whole family.
Do we need prior experience?
No prior knowledge is required — the program helps new families get started easily.
What about the costs?
Scouting is competitively priced compared to other youth organizations, and there are ways to make the program affordable for families who need assistance. No youth is turned away due to a family's inability to pay.
Are parents involved?
Yes! Cub Scouting is fun for the whole family. There is a place for every parent, guardian, or caring adult who wants to help.
